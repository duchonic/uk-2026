📍 Dalgety Bay on Maps
🏠 Base — Dalgety Bay, Fife
Dalgety Bay
A coastal town on the north shore of the Firth of Forth in Fife, Scotland. Just 25 miles from Edinburgh across the iconic Forth bridges, it offers a quiet base with easy access to both Edinburgh and the East Lothian coastline. The seafront promenade and views across the Forth are beautiful in summer.

🍽️ Dinner options — Edinburgh
Timberyard Restaurant
Award-winning sustainable Scottish cooking in a converted Victorian warehouse in the Grassmarket. Seasonal, locally sourced — genuinely excellent.
Café St Honoré Bistro
Charming French-Scottish bistro tucked down a New Town lane. Classic dishes, excellent wine list, and a warm neighbourhood atmosphere.
The Hanging Bat Pub
Edinburgh's beloved craft beer bar on Lothian Road — huge tap selection, great burgers, and lively atmosphere. No booking needed.
Divino Enoteca Wine bar
Italian enoteca on Merchant Street with 200+ wines by the glass or bottle. Excellent charcuterie and cheeses — ideal for a relaxed evening in the Old Town.
The Bow Bar Whisky bar
A legendary Edinburgh institution on West Bow — over 300 single malts, no music, no food. Just serious whisky and conversation. The definitive Scottish whisky bar.

🏰 Day trip — Dunbar
Dunbar, East Lothian
One of Scotland's sunniest towns, Dunbar has a picturesque harbour with ruined castle walls, the birthplace of conservationist John Muir, and access to the stunning East Lothian coast. The John Muir Country Park offers beach walks and wildlife spotting.

Things to explore — by interest
⚔️ Nicolas — Vikings Norse history near Edinburgh & Fife
National Museum of Scotland Edinburgh city centre · free entry World-class Viking gallery with swords, brooches, and the Galloway Hoard — one of the finest Viking-age treasure finds ever unearthed in Britain.
Museum of Edinburgh Royal Mile, Edinburgh · free Local history museum with Norse-era artefacts covering Viking settlement around the Firth of Forth and early medieval Edinburgh.
Inchcolm Island & Abbey Firth of Forth · ~40 min ferry from South Queensferry Island abbey with Viking-age carved stones. The Norsemen raided and used these islands repeatedly in the 9th–10th centuries. Stunning Forth views.
🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Steffi — Outlander filming locations Several sites within easy reach of Dalgety Bay
Culross ~20 min drive from Dalgety Bay · Fife Perfectly preserved 17th-century village used as Cranesmuir (Geillis Duncan's village) in Season 1. Cobbled streets and the Yellow Palace are instantly recognisable.
Midhope Castle ~30 min drive · West Lothian The exterior of Lallybroch (Broch Tuarach). Set in the Hopetoun Estate grounds — check access before visiting as it is on private land.
Doune Castle ~1 h drive · Stirling area Used as Castle Leoch (Clan MacKenzie seat) throughout the series. Also famous as Monty Python's Castle Camelot. Open to visitors year-round.
Falkland Village ~45 min drive · Fife Doubled as 1940s Inverness in the opening episodes. The village square and high street appear in several key scenes from Season 1.
Blackness Castle ~25 min drive · Falkirk area The grim Fort William where Jamie is flogged — a striking black fortress jutting into the Firth of Forth. Free Historic Scotland site.
⚡ Steffi — Harry Potter Edinburgh Where the books were born
The Elephant House George IV Bridge, Edinburgh · 30 min by train The café where J.K. Rowling wrote early chapters of the first book, overlooking Edinburgh Castle. A pilgrimage spot — walls of the toilets covered in fan messages.
Greyfriars Kirkyard Old Town, Edinburgh · next to The Elephant House The graveyard that inspired character names: look for the Tom Riddle headstone and the McGonagall grave. Also home to the famous Greyfriars Bobby story.
George Heriot's School Lauriston Place, Edinburgh · visible from Greyfriars The grand 17th-century school widely considered one of the main inspirations for Hogwarts — turrets, courtyards, and a house system eerily familiar to Potter fans.
Victoria Street Old Town, Edinburgh The colourful curved street that inspired Diagon Alley — winding, multi-coloured shopfronts and independent boutiques. Picturesque and very photogenic.
🥾 Walks & Trails
Easy
Dalgety Bay Coastal Trail
📏 4 miles (6.5 km)⏱ ~1 h 30↕ Mostly flat
A relaxed stroll along the Firth of Forth foreshore from Dalgety Bay to St David's Harbour and back. Sweeping views to Inchcolm Island, the Forth Bridges, and the Fife coastline. Good for birdwatching — look for oystercatchers and seals on the rocks. Entirely on maintained paths.
Medium
Fife Coastal Path — Dalgety Bay to Aberdour
📏 4.5 miles (7 km) one way⏱ ~1 h 45↕ Some undulation
Follow the Fife Coastal Path westward through Silver Sands Beach to the charming village of Aberdour — one of Scotland's few "blue flag" beaches. The path dips through woodland, clifftops, and a small castle ruin. Return easily on ScotRail (~10 min back to Dalgety Bay). Check tide times for the beach sections.
Medium
Arthur's Seat Loop — Edinburgh
📏 5 miles (8 km)⏱ ~2 h↕ 251 m summit — some steep rocky sections
The classic Edinburgh walk — an extinct volcano rising dramatically from Holyrood Park. The main ridge path from St Margaret's Loch is straightforward but gets rocky near the summit. Panoramic 360° views over the city, the Forth, and on clear days the Highlands. Start from the Holyrood Park car park. Sturdy shoes recommended.
