🏛️ Stop — York & Knaresborough
York
One of England's most historically rich cities, York was founded by the Romans (as Eboracum), became a Viking centre (Jorvik), and has a stunning medieval heritage. The Gothic Minster dominates the skyline, and the snaking medieval lanes of The Shambles are unmissable. Knaresborough nearby has one of England's most dramatic railway viaducts.

🍽️ Dinner options — York
Skosh Small plates
Consistently one of York's best restaurants — creative, globally influenced small plates in a relaxed Micklegate dining room. Very popular, book ahead.
Trembling Madness Pub
A medieval great hall transformed into one of England's most atmospheric bars. Hundreds of bottled beers, mead, and hearty food — right next to the Minster.
The Ivy York Brasserie
Reliable all-day brasserie on Stonegate in a beautiful listed building. Classic dishes, good cocktails, and a buzzing atmosphere — no surprises, always enjoyable.
Sotano Wine bar
Spanish-inspired wine bar beneath Stonegate — natural wines, sherries, and pintxos in a candlelit basement. One of York's most intimate spots for a pre- or post-dinner drink.
The Maltings Whisky bar
A traditional pub near Lendal Bridge with one of York's largest whisky collections. Unpretentious, friendly, and a favourite with locals — excellent cask ales too.

🌸 Harrogate
Harrogate, North Yorkshire
A refined Victorian spa town famed for its floral displays, elegant streets, and Betty's Café Tea Rooms — a Yorkshire institution since 1919. The Valley Gardens and Harlow Carr (RHS garden) are highlights. The Stray, a 200-acre expanse of open grassland, runs through the town centre.

🌿 Day trip — Ilkley Moor
Ilkley & Wharfedale
Ilkley Moor is the setting of Yorkshire's unofficial anthem and offers some of the best moorland walking in England. The Cow and Calf Rocks are iconic gritstone boulders with superb views down Wharfedale. The Victorian spa town of Ilkley has a Roman fort, excellent cafés, and the picturesque River Wharfe.

Things to explore — by interest
⚔️ Nicolas — Vikings York (Jórvík) was the capital of the Viking kingdom of Northumbria
Jorvik Viking Centre Coppergate, York · 30 min from Harrogate The UK's premier Viking attraction, built over the actual 10th-century Coppergate excavation. An immersive ride through reconstructed Viking Jórvík with authentic sounds and smells. Book ahead — always busy.
Yorkshire Museum Museum Gardens, York · free Houses the priceless Coppergate Helmet (8th century), Viking sword collection, and the York Hoard of 617 silver coins. One of England's most important Viking collections.
DIG: An Archaeological Adventure St Saviourgate, York York Archaeological Trust's hands-on dig site — unearth replica Viking, Roman, and medieval artefacts. More fun than it sounds, and great context before or after Jorvik.
York Minster & Viking foundations City centre, York The Minster's undercroft museum reveals Viking and Roman layers beneath the cathedral. The Vikings held their royal court here when York was Jórvík, capital of the Danelaw.
⚡ Steffi — Harry Potter in York Diagon Alley is right here
The Shambles York city centre · walking distance The medieval crooked street widely regarded as one of the key inspirations for Diagon Alley — overhanging timber-framed buildings, specialist shops, and a magical atmosphere. Now has a dedicated "Shambles Market" with HP merchandise stalls.
The Shambles Market Parliament Street, York Adjacent open-air market with Potterhead traders selling wands, robes, and collectibles. A fun browse even if you don't buy — several stalls run Harry Potter-themed activities.
🥾 Walks & Trails
Easy
Harrogate Valley Gardens & Pinewoods
📏 3.5 miles (5.5 km)⏱ ~1 h 20↕ Mostly flat
A gentle loop through Harrogate's finest green spaces — the ornate Victorian Valley Gardens (with its historic pump room and 36 mineral springs), across the Stray parkland, and into the peaceful Pinewoods. The perfect introduction to the spa town on arrival day. Entirely on good paths; the Tewit Well near the Stray is the oldest chalybeate spring in town.
Medium
Nidd Gorge & Knaresborough Riverside
📏 6 miles (9.5 km)⏱ ~2 h 30↕ Some woodland undulation, ~150 m total ascent
From Knaresborough station, follow the River Nidd downstream through the dramatic wooded gorge, past Mother Shipton's Cave (England's oldest visitor attraction) and the Dropping Well, before looping back along the opposite bank beneath the castle. Fantastic views of the viaduct from the riverbank. Return to Harrogate by train (~15 min).
Medium
Ilkley Moor — Cow & Calf Circular
📏 7 miles (11 km)⏱ ~3 h↕ ~330 m ascent — open moorland🚗 30 min drive or train to Ilkley
The definitive Yorkshire moors walk — climb from Ilkley to the iconic Cow and Calf rocks, then follow the moorland ridge with sweeping views across Wharfedale. The route continues over White Wells (a restored Georgian plunge bath) and back down through the gorge. Proper walking shoes essential; the moor can be boggy after rain. A proper Yorkshire day out.
