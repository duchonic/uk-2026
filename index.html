🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Scotland — near Edinburgh / Dalgety Bay
Stirling Castle ~45 min by train from Edinburgh
One of Scotland's most important castles, perched on a volcanic crag. Mary Queen of Scots was crowned here. Direct trains from Edinburgh Waverley.
Rosslyn Chapel ~35 min by bus from Edinburgh
The extraordinary 15th-century chapel made famous by The Da Vinci Code — but far more interesting in real life. Lothian Bus 37 runs directly.
Loch Lomond & The Trossachs ~1h 20 by train from Edinburgh
Scotland's first national park — dramatic loch scenery, Ben Lomond, and the charming village of Luss. Train to Balloch, then buses into the park.
St Andrews (full day) ~1h from Dalgety Bay (2 trains)
Already listed as a quick visit — but the medieval cathedral ruins, castle, and Old Course deserve a full day. Easiest from Dalgety Bay via Leuchars.
🏰 Northeast England — near Newcastle
Angel of the North ~20 min by Metro from Newcastle
Antony Gormley's iconic 20-metre steel sculpture. Take the Metro to Gateshead and a short walk. One of England's most visited artworks.
Alnwick Castle ~35 min by train + bus from Newcastle
The "Harry Potter castle" (exterior filming location) plus medieval state rooms and the extraordinary Alnwick Garden with its poison garden. Arriva X18 bus.
Bamburgh Castle ~1h from Newcastle (train + bus)
A dramatic Anglo-Saxon fortress on a coastal basalt crag, with long sandy beaches stretching below. Train to Chathill, then bus.
Holy Island / Lindisfarne ~1h 20 from Newcastle (train + bus)
Tidal island with a medieval priory, ruined castle, and extraordinary atmosphere. Check tide tables — the island is cut off twice daily. Bus from Berwick.
Hadrian's Wall ~45 min by bus from Newcastle
The best-preserved sections (Housesteads, Vindolanda) are 45 min by the AD122 Hadrian's Wall Bus (seasonal), which runs direct from Newcastle.
Beamish Open Air Museum ~30 min by bus from Newcastle
A brilliantly recreated 1900s and 1940s town — working trams, period shops, and a functioning colliery. Bus X71 from Newcastle.
🌊 North Yorkshire Coast — near Whitby
Staithes ~25 min by bus from Whitby
A strikingly atmospheric fishing village crammed into a steep narrow gorge — Captain Cook's first job was here. Arriva bus from Whitby.
Runswick Bay ~20 min by bus from Whitby
A curving sandy beach backed by a tumbledown village of red-roofed cottages. One of the most beautiful bays on the Yorkshire coast.
Rievaulx Abbey ~45 min by bus from Whitby
Hauntingly beautiful ruined Cistercian abbey in a wooded valley. One of England's finest medieval ruins — the scale is remarkable.
🌿 Yorkshire Dales — near Harrogate / Ilkley
Fountains Abbey & Studley Royal ~20 min by bus from Harrogate
UNESCO World Heritage Site — England's largest monastic ruin set within a stunning 18th-century water garden. Bus 139 from Harrogate. Do not miss.
Bolton Abbey ~15 min by bus from Ilkley
Romantic ruined Augustinian priory on the banks of the Wharfe, with beautiful woodland walks along the Strid. Wharfedale bus from Ilkley.
Skipton Castle ~30 min by train from Harrogate
One of England's best-preserved medieval castles, with a charming market town to explore. Direct trains from Harrogate on the Settle-Carlisle line.
Yorkshire Dales ~50 min by bus from Harrogate
Wensleydale (and its cheese), Swaledale, and Malham Cove — dramatic limestone scenery. Dales Bus services run seasonally from Harrogate.
🏛️ Southeast England — near St Albans / London
Cambridge ~45 min by train from St Albans
King's College Chapel, punting on the Cam, and the Fitzwilliam Museum. Train from St Albans City via London Farringdon to Cambridge.
Oxford ~1h by coach from London
The Bodleian Library, Christ Church, the Ashmolean Museum, and medieval lanes. Frequent Oxford Tube coaches from London Victoria.
Bath & the Roman Baths ~1h 20 by train from London
The only natural hot spring in Britain, with a perfectly preserved Roman bathing complex and Georgian architecture. Direct trains from Paddington.
Windsor Castle ~35 min by train from St Albans
The oldest and largest occupied castle in the world. Train to Windsor & Eton Central from London Paddington or via Slough.
Stonehenge ~1h 30 by train + bus from London
Train to Salisbury from London Waterloo, then the Stonehenge Tour bus. Best visited first thing in the morning before crowds arrive.
The Cotswolds ~1h 30 from London by train
Bourton-on-the-Water, Burford, and the Slaughters — honey-stone villages and rolling hills. Train to Moreton-in-Marsh from Paddington.
