🏠 Base — Dalgety Bay, Fife
Dalgety Bay
A coastal town on the north shore of the Firth of Forth in Fife, Scotland. Just 25 miles from Edinburgh across the iconic Forth bridges, it offers a quiet base with easy access to both Edinburgh and the East Lothian coastline. The seafront promenade and views across the Forth are beautiful in summer.
🌊 Firth of Forth views 🌉 Forth bridges nearby 🏰 Edinburgh ~25 mi 🇬🇧 Scotland

📍 Whitby on Maps
🦇 Arriving — Whitby
Whitby, North Yorkshire
A dramatic fishing port and former whaling town on the North Yorkshire coast, famous for its ruined clifftop abbey, 199 steps, and as the inspiration for Bram Stoker's Dracula. The harbour, old town, and stunning moors make it one of England's most atmospheric destinations.
🏚️ Whitby Abbey ⚓ Harbour 🦇 Dracula connection 🐟 Fish & chips 🌿 North York Moors

🏛️ Stop — York & Knaresborough
York
One of England's most historically rich cities, York was founded by the Romans (as Eboracum), became a Viking centre (Jorvik), and has a stunning medieval heritage. The Gothic Minster dominates the skyline, and the snaking medieval lanes of The Shambles are unmissable. Knaresborough nearby has one of England's most dramatic railway viaducts.
⛪ York Minster 🛤️ The Shambles ⚔️ Jorvik Viking Centre 🏰 York city walls 🌉 Knaresborough viaduct

🌸 Harrogate
Harrogate, North Yorkshire
A refined Victorian spa town famed for its floral displays, elegant streets, and Betty's Café Tea Rooms — a Yorkshire institution since 1919. The Valley Gardens and Harlow Carr (RHS garden) are highlights. The Stray, a 200-acre expanse of open grassland, runs through the town centre.

🌿 Day trip — Ilkley Moor
Ilkley & Wharfedale
Ilkley Moor is the setting of Yorkshire's unofficial anthem and offers some of the best moorland walking in England. The Cow and Calf Rocks are iconic gritstone boulders with superb views down Wharfedale. The Victorian spa town of Ilkley has a Roman fort, excellent cafés, and the picturesque River Wharfe.

🏛️ Arriving — St Albans
St Albans, Hertfordshire
One of England's oldest cities, built on the Roman town of Verulamium. The magnificent Norman cathedral is one of England's longest, and the city has a beautiful market square, independent boutiques, and excellent restaurants. Perfectly positioned for London day trips — just 20 minutes by train to St Pancras.
⛪ St Albans Cathedral 🏛️ Verulamium Roman ruins 🌿 Verulamium Park 🚂 20 min to London 🛍️ Market & boutiques
