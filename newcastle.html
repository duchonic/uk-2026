📍 Newcastle highlights
Things to see & do
Newcastle's quayside is one of England's most impressive urban waterfronts. The city is also the gateway to Hadrian's Wall and the beautiful Northumberland National Park. Don't miss the Grainger Market for local produce and the thriving café culture on Jesmond Road.

🍽️ Dinner options — Newcastle
House of Tides Fine dining
Kenny Atkinson's Michelin-starred restaurant in a 16th-century merchant's house on the Quayside. Outstanding modern British cooking — book well ahead.
Dobson & Parnell Bistro
Refined British brasserie on the Quayside in a beautifully restored warehouse space. Excellent seafood, great cocktails, relaxed atmosphere.
The Broad Chare Pub
Terry Laybourne's acclaimed Quayside pub with serious food and a stellar real ale selection. Steak and kidney pudding, potted crab — proper pub classics done brilliantly.
Barluga Wine bar
Stylish wine bar and brasserie on Grey Street — one of England's finest Victorian streets. Great wine list, cocktails, and light bites in an elegant setting.
The Centurion Whisky bar
A breathtaking Victorian bar inside Newcastle Central Station — high vaulted ceilings, stained glass, and an excellent spirits selection including a strong whisky range.

⛪ Day trip — Durham
Durham
A compact and stunning city dominated by its magnificent Norman cathedral and castle, both UNESCO World Heritage Sites. The medieval centre is largely pedestrianised and the River Wear creates a dramatic horseshoe loop around the historic peninsula. Only 12 minutes by train from Newcastle.
⛪ Durham Cathedral 🏰 Durham Castle 🌊 River Wear 🏛️ UNESCO World Heritage 🎓 Durham University
Things to explore — by interest
⚔️ Nicolas — Vikings Northumbria was the heart of the Danelaw
Great North Museum: Hancock Newcastle city centre · free entry Major regional museum with a dedicated gallery on Hadrian's Wall, Roman, and Viking-era Northumbria. Excellent Norse metalwork and coin hoards on display.
Bamburgh Castle ~1 h 20 north of Newcastle · Northumberland coast The royal seat of the Anglo-Saxon kingdom of Bernicia — raided repeatedly by Vikings from the 790s. The museum inside holds a stunning collection of Dark Age weaponry and armour.
Holy Island (Lindisfarne) ~1 h 30 north · accessible by tidal causeway Where Viking raids on Britain began in 793 AD — the Lindisfarne Gospels were created here. The priory ruins stand where the monks fled the Norse longships. Check tide times.
🥾 Walks & Trails
Easy
Newcastle Quayside & Seven Bridges Walk
📏 4 miles (6.5 km)⏱ ~1 h 30↕ Flat riverside
A flat, scenic loop along both banks of the Tyne, crossing the famous Millennium Bridge (the Blinking Eye), the Tyne Bridge, and the Swing Bridge. Pass the Baltic Centre for Contemporary Art, the Sage Gateshead, and lively quayside bars. Entirely paved — great for an evening stroll too.
Medium
Hadrian's Wall — Steel Rigg Circular
📏 5.5 miles (9 km)⏱ ~2 h 30↕ Rolling crags, ~200 m total ascent🚗 ~1 h drive west
The most dramatic stretch of Hadrian's Wall — walking the crests of the Great Whin Sill crags with the wall at your side. Pass Crag Lough lake and the iconic Sycamore Gap (the lone tree made famous by Robin Hood: Prince of Thieves). The loop returns via the Military Road. Park at Steel Rigg National Park car park (pay & display).
Medium
Tynemouth to St Mary's Lighthouse
📏 6 miles (9.5 km) one way⏱ ~2 h 15↕ Mostly flat coastal path🚇 Metro back from Whitley Bay
A superb North Sea coastal walk from Tynemouth Priory (Viking and Norman ruins) northward along the sandy beaches and rocky shoreline through Cullercoats Bay to the tidal island of St Mary's Lighthouse at Whitley Bay. Return on the Tyne & Wear Metro (Whitley Bay station). Check tide times for lighthouse access.
