🦇 Arriving — Whitby
Whitby, North Yorkshire
A dramatic fishing port and former whaling town on the North Yorkshire coast, famous for its ruined clifftop abbey, 199 steps, and as the inspiration for Bram Stoker's Dracula. The harbour, old town, and stunning moors make it one of England's most atmospheric destinations.

🌊 Whitby & surrounds
Top experiences
Whitby offers a perfect mix of history, coast, and moors. The old town on the east cliff has independent shops, galleries, and legendary fish & chip shops. The North Yorkshire Moors Railway runs steam trains to Pickering through stunning countryside. Nearby Robin Hood's Bay is one of Yorkshire's most photogenic spots.

🍽️ Dinner options — Whitby
The Star Inn the Harbour Restaurant
Andrew Pern's celebrated harbourside restaurant — outstanding Yorkshire seafood and seasonal produce, right on the water. Book ahead.
Trenchers Fish & chips
A step up from takeaway — full restaurant service with superb fresh fish & chips, local crab, and traditional puddings. A Whitby institution.
The Duke of York Pub
Famous old pub at the foot of the 199 steps — real ales, harbour views, and an atmosphere that hasn't changed in decades. The definitive Whitby pint.
The White Horse & Griffin Wine bar
A 17th-century coaching inn on Church Street with one of Whitby's best wine lists. Atmospheric stone interior and a relaxed bar — perfect for an evening glass after the abbey.
The Endeavour Whisky bar
Tucked on Church Street in the old town, The Endeavour carries an impressive selection of single malts alongside local ales — small, cosy, and full of maritime character.
Things to explore — by interest
⚔️ Nicolas — Vikings Whitby was a Norse settlement — the name itself is Old Norse
Whitby Museum Pannett Park · 10 min walk from town Local museum with Viking-era finds from the area, jet carvings, and artefacts from the Synod of Whitby (664 AD). The town's name "Whitby" derives directly from Old Norse Hvítabýr (white settlement).
Whitby Abbey East Cliff, above town · 199 steps The original monastery was razed by Viking raiders in 867 AD. The dramatic ruined Gothic abbey you see today was rebuilt after the Norse era — but its Viking destruction is a key part of the story.
Jorvik Viking Centre ~1 h drive to York — worth combining with Day 10 The UK's top Viking attraction — an immersive time-capsule ride through reconstructed Viking-age York (Jórvík). Book tickets in advance. Perfect to combine with the Whitby → York travel day.
🥾 Walks & Trails
Easy
Whitby to Sandsend Beach Walk
📏 3.5 miles (5.5 km) one way⏱ ~1 h 20↕ Flat beach & low clifftop
Head north from Whitby harbour along the beach or the clifftop path to the pretty village of Sandsend. The beach route is easy going at low tide; the clifftop alternative gives great views back to Whitby Abbey. Return on the Coastliner 840 bus or retrace the beach. A lovely evening walk.
Medium
Esk Valley — Whitby to Ruswarp Circular
📏 5 miles (8 km)⏱ ~2 h↕ Gentle river valley, one short hill
Leave Whitby via the old town and follow the River Esk upstream through meadows and woodland to the village of Ruswarp, returning via the opposite bank. Passes the NYMR steam railway bridge — good chance of catching a steam train. Mostly flat with one short climb back into Whitby through the churchyard above the 199 steps.
Medium
Cleveland Way — Whitby to Robin Hood's Bay
📏 6 miles (9.5 km) one way⏱ ~2 h 30↕ Rolling coastal clifftops, ~300 m total ascent
One of England's finest coastal walks — the Cleveland Way follows spectacular clifftops southward from Whitby with constant sea views and dramatic drops to the shore. Robin Hood's Bay is a picturesque fishing village crammed into a ravine. Return on the Arriva X93 bus (runs regularly back to Whitby). Book a coffee at the Bay Hotel at the finish.
