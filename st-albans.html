🏛️ Arriving — St Albans
St Albans, Hertfordshire
One of England's oldest cities, built on the Roman town of Verulamium. The magnificent Norman cathedral is one of England's longest, and the city has a beautiful market square, independent boutiques, and excellent restaurants. Perfectly positioned for London day trips — just 20 minutes by train to St Pancras.

🍽️ Dinner options — St Albans
Lussmans Fish & Grill Fish & grill
St Albans' most popular local restaurant — sustainably sourced fish and grills, great value, relaxed atmosphere. A local favourite for good reason.
Darcy's Bistro
Long-established French-British restaurant in an elegant 18th-century dining room steps from the cathedral. Classic cooking, excellent wine, and warm service.
The White Hart Tap Pub
Characterful old coaching inn near the cathedral with a superb cask ale selection and good honest pub food. One of St Albans' most atmospheric pubs.
Sucre Wine bar
Vibrant Latin-influenced bar and restaurant with an interesting wine list focused on South American and natural wines. Great cocktails and small plates — lively and fun.
The Boot Whisky bar
A venerable St Albans pub with one of the best whisky selections in Hertfordshire — dozens of malts alongside cask ales. Cosy, unhurried, and a short walk from the cathedral.

Things to explore — by interest
⚡ Steffi — Harry Potter The Studio Tour is basically next door
Warner Bros. Studio Tour — The Making of Harry Potter Leavesden, Watford · ~20 min drive from St Albans The original Great Hall, Dumbledore's office, Hogwarts Express, Diagon Alley set, and the full-scale Hogwarts model. Arguably the top HP experience in the world. Book well in advance — sells out weeks ahead.
Platform 9¾ — King's Cross King's Cross station, London · ~30 min by train The iconic trolley-through-the-wall photo spot inside King's Cross station, with a shop selling HP merchandise. Free to visit, but queues can be long — go early.
Leadenhall Market City of London · ~45 min by train The Victorian covered market used as the exterior of the Leaky Cauldron and the entrance to Diagon Alley in the films. Beautiful architecture even without the HP connection.
Australia House The Strand, London The Grand Banking Hall of Gringotts in the first two films. The building is not normally open to visitors but the exterior is easily photographed from The Strand.
⚔️ Nicolas — Vikings The British Museum has one of the world's greatest Viking collections
The British Museum — Room 41 (Vikings) Bloomsbury, London · free entry · 35 min by train Dedicated Viking gallery with the Lewis Chessmen, the Vale of York Hoard, runestones, weaponry, and jewellery. One of the most comprehensive Viking collections outside Scandinavia.
Museum of London Docklands Canary Wharf, London Covers London's Viking-era history including the famous "Battle of London Bridge" (1014) when Norse king Olaf Haraldsson tore the bridge down — possibly the origin of "London Bridge is Falling Down".
🥾 Walks & Trails
Easy
Verulamium Park & River Ver
📏 4 miles (6.5 km)⏱ ~1 h 30↕ Entirely flat
A gentle loop through one of England's finest Roman remains — the walls, the hypocaust mosaic (visible in situ), and the lake of Verulamium Park — then along the chalk stream of the River Ver through watercress beds and meadows. The Verulamium Museum on the route is excellent. Start and end in St Albans city centre.
Easy
Alban Way — St Albans to Hatfield
📏 6.5 miles (10.5 km) one way⏱ ~2 h 15↕ Flat (disused railway)
A completely flat, traffic-free path along the old Hatfield branch railway line through Hertfordshire countryside, parks, and woodland. The surface is good throughout — fine even in summer shoes. Return from Hatfield by train back to St Albans (~8 min). A peaceful alternative to the city on a sunny afternoon.
Medium
Chiltern Way — Sandridge & Nomansland Common
📏 7.5 miles (12 km)⏱ ~3 h↕ Gently rolling Hertfordshire countryside
A varied circular through the Hertfordshire countryside north of St Albans — open commons, hedgerow paths, and the pretty village of Sandridge (whose church dates to the 11th century). Nomansland Common is a wide expanse of gorse and grassland on the edge of the Chilterns AONB. Good underfoot in summer. Start from Marshalswick.
